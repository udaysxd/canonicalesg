# Governance Canonical Mapping Pack (CMP v0.1)

This Canonical Mapping Pack (CMP) documents illustrative interpretations of **governance-related disclosure intents** defined in Canonical Disclosure Intents (CDI v1).

It is provided as a **non-authoritative reference implementation**.

## Scope Declaration

This CMP documents illustrative interpretations of **sustainability-related governance disclosure intents**.

### Topic Scope
- Sustainability governance (board and management oversight)
- Governance structures, roles, processes, and policies

### Disclosure Intents Covered

- CDI-GOVR-01 — Board-level oversight of sustainability matters  
- CDI-GOVR-02 — Sustainability-related board committees  
- CDI-GOVR-03 — Management-level sustainability oversight  
- CDI-GOVR-04 — Assignment of sustainability governance responsibilities  
- CDI-GOVR-05 — Senior management responsibility for sustainability  
- CDI-GOVR-06 — Reporting lines for sustainability governance  
- CDI-GOVR-07 — Governance processes for sustainability oversight  
- CDI-GOVR-08 — Frequency of sustainability governance review  
- CDI-GOVR-09 — Integration of sustainability into organisational governance processes  
- CDI-GOVR-10 — Existence of sustainability governance policies  
- CDI-GOVR-11 — Scope of sustainability governance policies  
- CDI-GOVR-12 — Governance reporting boundary  
- CDI-GOVR-13 — Methodology for governance disclosures  
- CDI-GOVR-14 — Changes in governance structure or processes  

### Reporting Frameworks Referenced

- ESRS G1 (Governance)
- GRI 2 (General Disclosures – Governance)
- ISSB / IFRS Sustainability Governance
- TCFD / TNFD Governance pillars

### Intended Use

This CMP is illustrative only.  
It does **not** assert compliance, completeness, or authoritative interpretation.

---

## Interpretation Principles

- Disclosure intent is defined exclusively by CDI.
- Framework texts remain the authoritative source.
- Interpretive judgement is explicitly documented.
- Governance disclosures focus on structure and process, not effectiveness.
- No assessment of governance quality or outcomes is performed.

## Limitations and Known Gaps

- Governance effectiveness is not assessed.
- Ethics, integrity, and anti-corruption outcomes are excluded.
- Executive remuneration is not covered.
- Jurisdiction-specific governance requirements are excluded.
- Interpretations are illustrative and non-authoritative.
